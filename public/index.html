Closing Cost Options

You don't have to write a big
check to close — here's why.

🤝
Seller-Paid
Seller Concessions

In the right market, you negotiate for the seller to cover part or all of your closing costs. Written directly into your purchase offer — no cash needed from you at close.

Real ExampleOn a $400,000 home, 3% in seller concessions = ~$12,000 toward your closing costs. You bring less cash; the seller nets the same if the offer price is structured correctly.
📊
Rate Trade-Off
Lender Credits

You accept a slightly higher interest rate in exchange for the lender crediting your closing costs. Out-of-pocket at closing drops to near zero — monthly payment is modestly higher.

When It Makes SenseIf you plan to sell or refinance within 3–5 years, paying large closing costs upfront makes less sense than taking a small rate bump and keeping your cash.
💰
Cash at Closing
Pay Out of Pocket

Paying closing costs directly gives you the best available rate with no trade-offs. If you have the reserves and plan to stay long-term, this is almost always the lowest total cost.

Typical Range in AlaskaExpect 2–4% of the loan amount. On a $350,000 loan that's roughly $7,000–$14,000 in fees, taxes, prepaid items, and lender charges.
